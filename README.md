## What This Is

CS Made Easy is an introductory computer science textbook written by two high school students in Ethiopia. We wrote it because the students around us had no access to computers, no reliable internet, and no textbook that spoke to them directly. So we built one.

The book is designed to be fully self-contained — a student with no prior programming experience and no computer can work through it from cover to cover and come out able to think like a programmer. We tested that claim in a real course we ran ourselves, and the results gave us confidence that the approach works.

---

## Why We Wrote It

Most introductory CS resources assume you have a computer in front of you. Most assume you have internet. Many teach programming as a set of steps to memorize rather than a way of thinking to develop.

We wanted to write something different: a textbook that treats students as thinkers first and coders second. One that introduces every concept through a real problem, not a definition. One that works on paper, in a classroom, without any technology at all.

---

## Our Teaching Philosophy

The entire book is built on three principles we refuse to compromise:

**No memorization without understanding.** Every concept in this book is introduced through a problem that makes the concept necessary. Students encounter the need before they encounter the solution.

**No shortcuts around problem solving.** We do not give students templates to fill in. We give them problems to work through. The discomfort of not immediately knowing the answer is part of the learning.

**Students are trained to think, not copy.** A student who finishes this book properly should be able to sit in front of an unfamiliar problem, break it down logically, and make progress — even if they have never seen that problem before.

---

## What We Learned from CS50

This textbook was deeply shaped by what we learned from Harvard's CS50 — specifically Professor David Malan's approach to teaching. Six principles from that course became the backbone of our book:

- **Problem-driven learning** — concepts are introduced through real problems, not isolated definitions
- **Teaching through problem-solving** — students think through challenges step by step before seeing a solution
- **Real-world relevance** — programming is connected to how it is actually used in industry
- **Engaging storytelling** — abstract ideas are made concrete through narrative and analogy
- **Strong problem sets** — exercises are designed to build deep understanding, not reward memorization
- **Structured, consistent delivery** — every lesson follows a clear and repeatable format

CS50 showed us that education can be both rigorous and genuinely exciting. We tried to bring that same energy to students who would never get to take CS50 themselves.

---

## What We Tested

We ran a full pilot course using this textbook with 30 students. We taught the lessons, assigned the problem sets, and observed how students engaged with the material.

What we saw: students who entered the course relying on rote memorization left it approaching new problems with genuine reasoning. That was the goal. We believe we are moving in the right direction.

## What Is in This Repository

This repository contains the complete materials from our textbook and course:

**The Textbook** — the full written book, chapter by chapter, designed to be read and worked through without a computer

**Lecture Slides** — the visual materials we used when teaching each chapter in the classroom

**Lecture Photos** — documentation of the real classroom sessions where this material was tested

**Problem Sets (Psets)** — CS50-style challenge problems that accompany each chapter, designed to build deep understanding

**Assignments** — additional exercises for reinforcement and deeper application of each concept

## Our Role

We are not a content-sharing project. We are two students who wrote a book, taught a course, and are building something we hope will outlast us.

We are actively teaching students in real classrooms in Ethiopia. We are adapting university-level CS material to be accessible at the high school level. We are building foundational CS skills in a community that has had very little access to them. And we are designing a system — a textbook, a curriculum, a teaching approach — that other students and teachers can use long after we move on.
